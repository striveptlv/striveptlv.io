Common Questions

NEURO WELLNESS
FAQS

What makes this different from regular physical therapy? +
Traditional PT focuses on impairment-level treatment in a 1-on-1 setting. The Neuro Wellness Program is built for clients who've moved past that stage and want structured, progressive group training — similar to what a performance athlete would pursue after recovering from an injury. The focus is on building capacity, not just restoring function.
How do I know which class or program is right for my level? +
Each offering is labeled with an ambulatory level — from high-level ambulatory to limited household ambulator. During your intake, a STRIVE therapist will assess your capacity and place you in the appropriate class or program. Some clients attend multiple offerings across the week at different intensity tiers.
What's the difference between a "class" and a "wellness program"? +
Classes are scheduled group sessions you can attend on an ongoing basis — they follow a consistent structure with variation each session. Wellness programs (Neuro Strength & Conditioning and Adapted Mat Pilates) are structured cycles: you enroll, follow a periodized plan, and progress week over week toward defined goals. Programs require an intake and are not drop-in.
Why are groups kept so small? +
Neurological training requires precision. Small groups (4–5 max) allow the therapist to monitor movement quality, provide appropriate cueing, and adjust loading or assistance in real time — things that simply aren't possible in large fitness classes. Every client gets meaningful attention in every session.
Do I need to be referred by a physician? +
Physician referral is not required to participate in the Neuro Wellness Program. However, all clients complete a STRIVE intake screening before joining any class or program to ensure appropriate placement and safety. Contact us to schedule your screening appointment.
