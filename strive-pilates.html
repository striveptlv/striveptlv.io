The Method

Pilates Meets Physical Therapy

Pilates was originally developed by Joseph Pilates as a system of controlled, intentional movement emphasizing breath, postural alignment, and motor precision. What makes it extraordinary for neurological patients is that these exact principles align with what we know about neuroplasticity and motor re-learning.

"The mind, when housed within a healthful body, possesses a glorious sense of power." — Joseph Pilates

At STRIVE, our physical therapists apply the Pilates framework through a clinical lens — selecting, modifying, and sequencing movements based on your specific neurological presentation. This is not generic Pilates. This is movement medicine.

One of the most important things to understand about Pilates for neurological patients is its radical accessibility. Pilates does not require you to stand, to have full use of your limbs, or to move without assistance. Every exercise has a seated, supine, or supported variation. Patients with complete SCI, significant hemiplegia, or severe MS-related fatigue can participate meaningfully — because Pilates meets the body where it is, not where it used to be.

For patients with spinal cord injury, stroke, or demyelinating disorders like multiple sclerosis, pilates offers something rare in rehabilitation: a system that builds body awareness, neuromuscular control, and breath-driven stability without placing excessive demand on fatigued or impaired systems.

Concentration Full mental focus on each movement activates neural pathways that support motor re-learning — especially critical after stroke or incomplete SCI.
Centering Deep core and postural muscle activation improves trunk stability — the foundation of every transfer, standing activity, and gait cycle.
Control Deliberate, quality movement over quantity — teaching the nervous system new movement patterns through repetition and precision, not effort alone.
Breath Coordinated breathing regulates autonomic tone, supports spasticity management, and improves oxygen delivery to tissues with reduced circulation from neurological injury.
Flow & Precision Smooth, sequenced transitions reduce compensatory movement patterns — essential for patients working to re-establish normal motor programs after neural injury.
Who We Serve

Designed for Neurological Diagnoses

These sessions are built around the specific presentations, challenges, and goals common to three neurological populations — not adapted from a general fitness template.

01

Spinal Cord Injury

For incomplete and complete SCI patients at any level, Pilates principles support trunk and respiratory function, activate preserved or recovering motor pathways, and improve postural control in sitting and supported standing.

Trunk stability and respiratory muscle activation
Neuroplasticity-focused repetition in preserved motor segments
Spasticity modulation through controlled stretch and breath
Seated and supported standing formats for all levels
Skin integrity awareness integrated throughout sessions
02

Stroke & Hemiplegia

Post-stroke patients benefit from Pilates' emphasis on bilateral awareness, weight-shifting, and midline orientation — all critical for addressing asymmetrical movement patterns and rebuilding cortical motor maps.

Bilateral body awareness and midline orientation
Weight-bearing through the affected upper and lower extremities
Neglect and hemianopia accommodation strategies
Upper extremity dissociation and shoulder girdle stability
Fatigue-paced programming for post-stroke fatigue syndrome
03

Demyelinating Disorders

For patients with multiple sclerosis, Guillain-Barré syndrome (GBS), functional movement disorder (FMD) and other neurological conditions; sessions are paced with Uhthoff's phenomenon and fatigue management as core clinical priorities.

Heat and fatigue management — cool environment, energy pacing
Coordination and proprioceptive training for MS ataxia
Cognitive-motor dual-task training where appropriate
Bladder and bowel precaution integration
Relapse-sensitive programming with rest period protocols

Is This Right for You?

Who Benefits Most

These sessions complement — and are often sequential to — formal physical therapy. They are designed for patients who are medically stable and motivated to maintain and build on their rehabilitation gains.

You may be a great fit if you…

  • Have completed acute or post-acute PT and want to continue progressing
  • Are managing a chronic neurological diagnosis and want structured, safe movement
  • Want a supervised, clinically-informed wellness session between PT visits
  • Are in the community phase of SCI recovery and have functional sitting balance
  • Have MS or NMOSD and want fatigue-smart exercise outside of formal rehab
  • Have post-stroke functional gains and want to reinforce motor learning through practice
  • Want body awareness, breath-work, and postural training as part of your wellness routine

We will evaluate first if you have…

  • Recent neurological event — acute or subacute stroke within 3 months, recent SCI hospitalization
  • Active autonomic dysreflexia episodes that are not yet managed
  • Significant hemodynamic instability with positional change
  • Active relapse of MS, GBS, FMD, or other neurological diagnosis
  • Medical complexity requiring a physician clearance — we will coordinate with your team

A note on medical screening

Every new participant completes a clinical intake with a STRIVE physical therapist before their first session. This is not optional — it is how we ensure your sessions are safe, appropriate, and purposefully designed. We may coordinate with your neurologist or physiatrist as needed.
