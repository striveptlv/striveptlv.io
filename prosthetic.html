What Drives Every Session

Two Phases.
One Continuum.

Phase One

Pre-Prosthetic Training

Before a prosthesis is fitted, the work begins. We focus on residual limb care and shaping, strengthening the surrounding muscles, improving balance, and building the endurance needed to tolerate and use a device well. We coordinate directly with your prosthetist at ForMotion, Hanger, Nevada Orthotics & Prosthetics, or Nevada Limb & Brace to ensure your physical preparation and device fitting are moving together.

Phase Two

Prosthetic Training

Once fitted, learning to use a prosthesis is its own skill. We work closely alongside your prosthetics team to align how the device fits with how you move — adjusting training as your socket fit, comfort, and function evolve. Gait, weight-bearing, terrain skills, and daily life use are all built in partnership with your prosthetist.

Areas of Focus

What We Work On

Every program is shaped around your amputation level, your device, and the life you are working toward.

Residual Limb Conditioning

Desensitization, shaping, and skin care to prepare the limb for a well-fitting, comfortable prosthesis.

Strength & Conditioning

Targeted strengthening of the hip, core, and surrounding musculature to support stable and efficient movement.

Balance & Fall Prevention

Single-leg and dynamic balance training to build steadiness and reduce fall risk at home and in the community.

Gait Training

Step-by-step progression from parallel bars to open environments — walking with a prosthesis takes practice and cues.

Transfers & Mobility

Safe strategies for getting in and out of bed, chairs, cars, and the floor — with and without your prosthesis.

Community & Terrain Skills

Curbs, ramps, stairs, and uneven ground — we practice the real surfaces you'll encounter outside the home.
