Strive Talk was engineered around one question: what does a patient with mobility limitations actually need to communicate freely?

Tap-to-Speak
One tap on any card and the app speaks it aloud — instantly, naturally. No typing. No menus. No delay.
Fully Customizable Cards
Build a personal library of words and phrases. Color-code by category. Add emojis for fast visual recognition.
Single-Hand Operation
Every interaction is designed for one finger, one thumb, or even a stylus. No pinching, no swiping, no two-handed combos.
Offline Support
Designed to keep key communication cards available after setup, so weak connections do not stop every interaction.
Voice Customization
13 voice languages, adjustable pitch and speed. Settings are remembered automatically — set it and forget it.
Privacy-Minded
Built to minimize setup friction and keep communication simple without asking patients to manage a complicated account flow.
Accidental-Delete Protection
Cards can only be removed in a deliberate delete mode — essential for users with motor control challenges.

THE PROBLEM

With traditional AAC device

Every day in the clinic, we see the same story: patients who need to communicate, stuck fighting their tools instead of focusing on recovery. Part of the problem is cost: many traditional AAC devices run $3,000-$8,000.

Too heavy to carry
Patients already managing wheelchairs, walkers, and mobility aids don't need another bulky device weighing them down.
Demands two hands
Most AAC hardware assumes full fine motor control — exactly what stroke and mobility patients have lost.
Creates social stigma
Carrying a dedicated medical device signals disability in every room. That stigma stops patients from using the tools they need.
Costs thousands
Dedicated AAC devices can cost $3,000-$8,000. Insurance coverage is uncertain. Cost should never determine whether someone has a voice.
Complex to set up
Long setup processes, professional programming requirements, and confusing interfaces frustrate patients and caregivers.
Fails without power or Wi-Fi
Many solutions require internet connections or frequent charging. Communication needs don't pause for tech problems.
From the Creator

As a therapist, I see patients every single day who need to communicate but are let down by the tools available to them. Strive Talk was built to remove every barrier I could see — cost, complexity, bulk, and stigma — so patients can stop fighting their device and start focusing on what matters: being heard.
