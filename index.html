Services for complex conditions

STRIVE's care is designed for adults who need thoughtful, individualized neurological and vestibular treatment. Every service is delivered one-on-one in your Las Vegas home.

Neurological Rehabilitation

Comprehensive recovery support for stroke, traumatic brain injury, Parkinson's disease, multiple sclerosis, and other neurological conditions affecting mobility, balance, and independence.

Vestibular Rehabilitation

Evidence-based treatment for dizziness, BPPV, vestibular loss, imbalance, chronic motion sensitivity, and related visual-vestibular symptoms.

Wellness Services

Support strength, endurance, long-term mobility, and confidence with personalized wellness-focused care after traditional therapy ends.

Concussion & Brain Injury

Targeted care for post-concussion symptoms including vestibulo-ocular issues, activity intolerance, and the overlap between brain injury and balance dysfunction.

Post-Amputation Rehabilitation

Residual limb care, desensitization, strength building, gait re-education, and functional progression with or without a prosthesis.

Functional Neurologic Disorder (FND)

Thoughtful, individualized care for Functional Neurological Disorder with a focus on listening well, pacing appropriately, and building meaningful daily independence.

Specialized stroke rehab delivered at home

STRIVE Physical Therapy provides 1-on-1 stroke rehabilitation in Las Vegas for adults recovering from ischemic stroke, hemorrhagic stroke, and other neurological events affecting mobility, walking, balance, and independence.

Because treatment happens in the home, every visit is shaped around the real tasks that matter: transfers, stairs, turning, bathroom safety, walking endurance, community mobility, and confidence in everyday environments.

For many patients, the problem is not that therapy never started. It is that traditional therapy stopped before progress felt complete. STRIVE is designed to extend recovery with focused, private, high-attention care.

Walking and Balance Recovery Targeted retraining for gait, turning, stairs, coordination, and fall-risk reduction.
In-Home Functional Training Practice movement in the exact spaces where daily life happens.
Licensed Stroke Specialists CSRS-certified providers with deep neurological rehab experience.
Caregiver Guidance Clear education for families supporting safety, mobility, and follow-through.

Who We Help After Stroke

Weakness, slowed walking, or loss of endurance after a recent or past stroke

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a doctor's referral to start PT in Nevada?+
In Nevada, physical therapists can evaluate and treat patients without a physician referral for most conditions. For our current cash-pay in-home services, no referral is needed.
Where in Las Vegas do you provide in-home therapy?+
We serve patients throughout the greater Las Vegas valley including Skye Canyon, Centennial Hills, Summerlin, and surrounding communities. Reach out if you want to confirm your specific area.
How is STRIVE different from a home health agency?+
Home health PT is typically designed for homebound patients who need basic functional support. STRIVE is built for patients who want more focused, higher-attention neurological and vestibular care in the home.
What do CSRS and CBIS certifications mean?+
CSRS and CBIS are advanced certifications that reflect deeper knowledge and clinical competency in stroke rehabilitation and brain injury care.
Can BPPV be treated in one visit?+
Many cases of BPPV respond quickly to repositioning maneuvers such as the Epley maneuver. The exact number of visits depends on the type of BPPV and what we find during evaluation.
When will your Las Vegas outpatient clinic open?+
We are actively working toward opening a Las Vegas outpatient neuro movement gym and clinic. Use the contact form below to join the notification list.
How long are sessions and how often will I be seen?+
Initial evaluations are typically 60–75 minutes. Follow-up sessions are usually 45–60 minutes. Frequency depends on your diagnosis, goals, and how your plan progresses.
Can I use my HSA or FSA card?+
Yes. Physical therapy is generally an eligible HSA/FSA expense. We can also provide superbills for potential out-of-network reimbursement depending on your plan.
