What This Stroke Rehab Is Built For

STRIVE Physical Therapy provides stroke physical therapy in Las Vegas for adults recovering from ischemic stroke, hemorrhagic stroke, and other neurological events that affect strength, coordination, balance, walking, and day-to-day independence.

Many people searching for stroke rehab in Las Vegas or stroke PT in Las Vegas are not looking for more generic maintenance. They want more time, more challenge, and more attention to the actual tasks that shape daily independence.

Recovery does not have to stop when standard rehab ends. Many stroke survivors still have meaningful gains available when treatment is specific, progressive, and tied to real-life goals.

If you or a loved one are searching for stroke rehab near Summerlin, Centennial Hills, Skye Canyon, or surrounding Las Vegas communities, this page is here for exactly that need: private stroke rehab that is built around real functional goals and delivered with close clinical attention.

Common goals we address:

  • Walk farther, faster, or with better control after stroke
  • Improve balance and reduce fear of falling during daily life
  • Get stronger with targeted work for the affected side
  • Practice transfers, stairs, bed mobility, and bathroom safety
  • Return to errands, appointments, restaurants, and outings
  • Keep progressing after traditional PT has plateaued

Every stroke recovery path is different. The plan should reflect that.

What We Work On

Stroke Rehab Goals We Pursue Together

Our treatment is built around functional progress, not just maintenance. The goal is to help you move better where life actually happens.

Walking

Gait Recovery

Gait training, turning, stair negotiation, endurance building, and more efficient movement after stroke.

Safety

Balance & Fall Recovery

Reactive balance work, confidence building, and learning how to recover from the floor when clinically appropriate.

Strength

Affected-Side Strengthening

Targeted work to improve force production, endurance, and confidence in the arm or leg affected by stroke.

Transfers

Home Mobility

Transfer training, bed mobility, sit-to-stand practice, bathroom safety, and movement through the home with better control.

Capacity

Endurance & Daily Function

Building capacity for longer walks, busier days, and more activity without feeling trapped by early fatigue or deconditioning.

Community

Real-World Independence

Practice for errands, appointments, restaurants, curb negotiation, and other community tasks that matter to your actual life.
