Why choose private pay physical therapy?

Because after a stroke, brain injury, FND diagnosis, MS flare, or other neurological change, the therapy model matters. STRIVE gives Las Vegas patients focused 1-on-1 neurological physical therapy built around your home, your goals, and the recovery window you are in right now.

The usual problem

Most outpatient PT was not designed for complex neuro recovery.

Insurance-based clinics are often staffed by skilled, caring therapists working inside a volume-based system. That system can make neurological rehabilitation harder than it needs to be.

Divided attention

Many clinics schedule 2 to 4 patients at the same time. For neuro rehab, subtle changes in gait, coordination, fatigue, and safety need real-time observation.

Ortho-first settings

Stroke, TBI, FND, MS, vestibular disorders, and balance changes require different reasoning than knee replacements or shoulder pain.

Visit caps and delays

Prior authorizations, yearly visit limits, and coverage denials can interrupt treatment during the very months when repetition and intensity matter most.

Transportation drain

Parking, waiting rooms, transfers, fatigue, and long rides can use up energy before therapy even begins. STRIVE brings therapy to the place you actually live.

Neuroplasticity rewards focused practice, not fragmented minutes.

Recovery is shaped by intensity, repetition, specificity, safety, and consistency. Those are hard to protect when your therapist is split between patients.

Why it works

The science favors intensity, repetition, and specificity.

Neurological recovery is driven by practice that is challenging, meaningful, specific to the task, and repeated enough to help the nervous system adapt.

1

Higher intensity matters

Longer, more focused sessions create more opportunities for meaningful repetitions and skilled feedback.

2

The recovery window matters

The first 3-6 months after stroke or neurological injury can be a peak period for neuroplastic change, but progress can continue beyond that with the right plan.

3

Real tasks transfer better

Walking to your bathroom, getting into your car, cooking dinner, or navigating your stairs requires practice in the context where life happens.

Questions to ask before you start PT.

Whether you choose STRIVE or another clinic, these questions help you understand whether the care model fits neurological recovery.

How many of your current patients have neurological diagnoses like stroke, brain injury, FND, or MS?
How do you keep my care plan consistent if scheduling, PTO, or vacations affect provider availability?
How many patients will my therapist treat at the same time as me?
What neuro rehab training, continuing education, or certifications do you hold?
How will you measure and track my progress over time?
Can therapy happen at home, where my actual daily challenges happen?
